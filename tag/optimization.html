Positive definite matrix

Date Wed 09 January 2019 By Nekoo Category Math. Tags Math Optimization

Definition1

In linear algebra, a symmetric \(n \times n\) real matrix \(M\) is said to be positive definite if the scalar \(z^{T}Mz\) is strictly positive for every non-zero column vector \(z\) of \(n\) real numbers. Here \(z^{\textsf {T}}\) denotes the transpose of \(z\).
